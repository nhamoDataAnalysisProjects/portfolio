Excel Project: Bike Dataset

Summary: This project examines a bike dataset to analyze usage patterns, seasonal trends, and factors influencing ridership. I chose this dataset to showcase my ability to derive actionable insights for transportation planning and urban mobility solutions.

Objective:: A bike dataset provides structured data to analyze trends and patterns in bike usage, rentals, or sales. It helps optimize operations, such as fleet management or inventory, and supports business decisions like pricing and service improvements. Additionally, it can guide urban planning by identifying cycling behavior and infrastructure needs.

Data source: link to dataset

Process: As a data analyst working with Excel, I started preparing the data through cleaning, handling missing values, and standardizing formats using functions like TRIM, CLEAN, and SUBSTITUTE by removing extra spaces, unwanted characters, or replacing text. I also leveraged IFERROR, COUNTIF, and lookup functions (e.g., VLOOKUP, XLOOKUP) to identify duplicates, and transform datasets for analysis. Using formulas, pivot tables, and charts for analysis, and automate repetitive tasks with advanced functions. Finally, I created interactive dashboards using slicers, conditional formatting, and annotated visualizations to present key insights effectively.

Results: Middle-aged individuals, particularly those between the ages of 32 and 53, are the most frequent bike buyers. Among those who purchased bikes, the majority reported riding between 0 and 1 mile. Additionally, the average income of male and female bike owners is relatively similar, indicating no significant income disparity between genders in bike ownership.
