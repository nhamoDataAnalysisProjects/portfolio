SQL Project: Layoffs Dataset

Summary: This project analyzes a layoff dataset to uncover trends, patterns, and key factors driving workforce reductions across industries. I chose this dataset to gain insights into a timely and impactful topic, showcasing my ability to interpret data and address real-world challenges relevant to business decision-making.

Objective:: The objective of using a layoffs dataset is to analyze trends and patterns in workforce reductions across industries, companies, or time periods. This data helps identify factors leading to layoffs, such as economic conditions or sector-specific challenges, and assess their impact on the labor market. Insights from the dataset can support workforce planning, policy-making, and strategies for mitigating future layoffs.

Data source: link to dataset.

Process: I performed exploratory data analysis (EDA) and statistical tests and the use of CTEs. To analyze a dataset about layoffs in SQL, I imported the dataset into a SQL database and performing data cleaning tasks such as removing duplicates and handling missing values. Next, I utilized SQL queries to explore the data, identifying key metrics like the number of layoffs per department or time period, and use aggregation functions to calculate summary statistics. Finally, I created visualizations in Tableau and reports using SQL to present my findings and insights effectively to stakeholders.

Results: Katerra, a company in the SF Bay Area, had the largest single-day layoffs, letting go of 2,434 employees. Amazon experienced the highest total layoffs between 2020 and 2024, with 27,840 employees affected, leading layoffs in 2023 (17260), while Intel topped 2024 (15062), Meta in 2022 with 11000, ByteDance in 2021 (3600), and Uber in 2020. The United States saw the highest number of layoffs globally, with the retail industry hit particularly hard during this period.
