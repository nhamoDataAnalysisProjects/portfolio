Python Project: Movie Dataset

Summary: This project explores a movie dataset to identify trends in box office performance, genre popularity, and audience preferences. I chose this dataset to demonstrate my analytical skills while working on a subject that combines business insights with entertainment industry dynamics.

Objective:: The objective of using a movie dataset as a data analyst is to extract insights about trends, patterns, and performance in the film industry. My goal is to explore metrics such as box office revenue, viewer ratings, genres, and release dates to understand audience preferences and predict success factors. This data supports decision-making for studios, streaming platforms, and marketing teams, helping optimize content creation, distribution strategies, and promotional efforts.

Data source: link to dataset

Process: To analyze the movie dataset in Python, I imported libraries like pandas for data manipulation and matplotlib or seaborn for visualizations, then loaded and explored the dataset to understand its structure and handle missing values or duplicates. I performed descriptive analysis and visualize the correlation, such as average ratings, genre popularity, or correlations like budget vs. revenue.

Results: A high or positive correlation between columns like gross revenue and budget indicates that films with larger budgets tend to generate higher revenue. Similarly, a positive correlation between votes and budget suggests that higher-budget films often attract more audience engagement, leading to more votes or popularity.
